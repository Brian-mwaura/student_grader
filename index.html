TOUGH DRILLS

PLANK

Planks work the core muscles, including the abdominals and lower back. To perform a plank, individuals rest on their forearms and toes while maintaining a straight line from the head to the heels. Holding this position for 30 seconds to a minute can greatly improve core stability and overall strength. Variations of the plank, such as side planks or plank jacks, can add further challenge and engage additional muscle groups.

BURPEES

Burpees are a dynamic full-body drill that combines cardio and strength training. They involve quickly dropping to a push-up position, performing a push-up, jumping the feet back towards the hands, and then explosively jumping into the air with arms raised. Burpees target multiple muscle groups, including the arms, chest, legs, and core. They provide an excellent cardiovascular workout while also building strength and endurance.

LUNGES

Lunges are a versatile gym drill that targets the major muscles in the lower body, including the quads, glutes, and hamstrings. To perform a lunge, individuals take a big step forward with one leg, lowering the body until both knees are at a 90-degree angle. They then push off the front leg to return to the starting position and switch legs. Lunges can be done stationary or while moving forward or backward, making them an effective exercise for improving lower body strength, balance, and coordination.

PUSH-UPs

push-up is a classic gym drill that targets the muscles in the chest, shoulders, and arms. To perform a push-up, individuals start in a high plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart and lower the body until the chest nearly touches the ground. They then push back up to the starting position. Push-ups can be modified by performing them on the knees or against a wall to reduce the load, making them accessible for beginners or individuals with limited upper body strength.

BOX JUMPS

Box jumps are a popular gym drill that focuses on explosive power and lower body strength. This exercise involves jumping from a stationary position onto a raised platform, such as a plyometric box, and then immediately jumping back down. Box jumps engage the muscles in the calves, quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, while also improving coordination and balance. It is important to start with a lower height and gradually increase the challenge as confidence and strength build.
